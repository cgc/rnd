 T R A V E L L E R ' S  G A T E S
----------------------------------

The depth listed includes the boss chamber at the bottom.
The maximum size applies to levels with narrow passages
between screens.  "Big Room" levels, which can appear in
any Gate, are always 4x4 screens, and special levels
(lost woods, ice, etc.) are also of fixed sizes.

The monster encounters for each level range are listed
in descending order of frequency--that is, the first
monster listed is the most likely to appear.  Monsters
marked with a * will only appear alone, and never with
other monsters, whether of the same or another species.

Two of the Gates (the Medal Man's Gate and Wisdom) seem to
have encounter tables which are never actually used.
This is probably a bug, but it's possible that Enix changed
their minds during development as to which monsters should
be encountered in these Gates.

The final Gate listed is apparently one which Enix intended
to add to the game, but ran out of development time and/or
cartridge space.  It can only be entered with the aid of a
GameShark code.  If you reach the bottom, the boss chamber
is unoccupied, but it looks similar to the location where
you fought DarkDrium in the SFC game Dragon Quest 6.

 N P C  M O N S T E R  M A S T E R S
-------------------------------------

The monster team used by an NPC Monster Master
encountered in a Gate depends on the sum of the levels
of your team.

Unlike normal wild monsters, the monsters on NPC Monster
Masters' teams possess skills other than the three
intrinsic skills of their species.  If you befriend one,
it will learn any intrinsic skills it may be "missing"
once it gains a level.

 M O N S T E R  D A T A
------------------------

FA: Family
0 - Slime  1 - Dragon 2 - Beast  3 - Bird   4 - Plant
5 - Bug    6 - Devil  7 - Zombie 8 - Mater. 9 - Boss

ML: Max Level
The Max Level of a wild-caught monster is this value +/- 2 (random)
The Max Level of a bred monster is this value + Plus * 2

XP: XP/Level table
This is a pointer to one of 32 different XP tables.
In general, the higher the number, the more XP the
monster needs per level:
00-07 = Fast      (2 XP for level 2)
08-15 = Normal    (5 XP for level 2)
16-23 = Slow      (10 XP for level 2)
24-31 = Very slow (100 XP for level 2)

SX: Sex probability
This determines the sex of a monster hatched from an egg.
The sex of a wild-caught monster may depend on where it is caught
(for example, all the catchable Gate bosses are of predetermined sex)
Note that any egg's sex can be changed by having it BLESSed.
0 = Always male
1 = 9/10 male
2 = Even chance
3 = 5/6 female

FL: Flying monster?
If this is 1, the monster locomotes exclusively by flying.
Flying monsters are immune to "LegSweep" and "BigTrip".

ME: Metal Slime family?
If this is 1, the monster is a member of the Metal Slime family.
Metal Slimes are susceptible to "MetalCut", and non-player ones
are more likely to run away than normal monsters.  The AI seems
to use slightly different criteria than normal when choosing
a target for an attack or recovery skill when one of the
monsters in question is a Metal Slime.

Note that the GoldSlime is not considered to be a member of the
Metal Slime family.

HP: HP Growth rating
MP: MP Growth rating
AT: ATK Growth rating
DF: DEF Growth rating
AG: AGL Growth rating
IN: INT Growth rating
See the "Growth" section for an explanation of these ratings.

The following 26 classes of special attack exist in DWM:

A: Blaze (etc.), FireSlash, BigBang, FireStaff
B: Firebal (etc.), LavaStaff
C: Bang (etc.)
D: Infernos (etc.), VacuSlash, MultiCut, WindBeast, Vacuum, WindStaff
E: Bolt (etc.), BoltSlash, Lightning, Hellblast, BoltStaff
F: IceBolt (etc.), IceSlash, SnowStaff
G: Surround, SandStorm, Radiant
H: Sleep, NapAttack, SleepAir
I: Beat (etc.), K.O.Dance, EerieLite, UltraDown
J: RobMagic (etc.), OddDance, RobDance
K: StopSpell, MistStaff
L: PanicAll, PaniDance
M: Sap, SickLick (DEF down)
N: Slow (AGL down)
O: Sacrifice, Ramming, Kamikaze
P: MegaMagic
Q: FireAir (etc.)
R: IceAir (etc.)
S: PoisonHit, PoisonGas, PoisonAir, BadMeat
T: Paralyze, PalsyAir
U: Curse
V: Ahhh, LureDance, LushLicks, LegSweep, BigTrip, WarCry (Miss-a-Turn)
W: DanceShut
X: MouthShut
Y: CallHelp, YellHelp, RockThrow
Z: GigaSlash

Resistance to these attacks is a species characteristic and
ranges from 0 to 3, 0 being completely non-resistant and 3
being (almost) completely immune.

When a monster is bred, it has a chance to inherit resistances
from its parents that are higher than the norm for its species.
The chance of inheriting an exceptional resistance is related
to the new monster's "plus" value.  You can EVALuate an egg to
find out whether the monster inherited an exceptional
resistance or not, but not what exceptional resistance was
inherited.

 G R O W T H
-------------

The growth rate of a monster's stats is determined by this
table.  The columns of the table correspond to the stat
values (from 0 to 31) in the monster data table, and the rows
indicate the cumulative points gained up to each level (to
make it easier to compare monsters' strengths and weaknesses).
To find the actual number of points gained at a particular
level, subtract the previous row from the row corresponding
to the level you're interested in.

Note that growth of HP and ATK (and only those two stats) is
affected by a monster's "plus".  This is why metal slimes may
sometimes gain more than 1 HP per level.

The table has been broken up into 16 column by 20 row sections
in order to be more readable on 80x24 terminals.

 P E R S O N A L I T Y
-----------------------

A monster's personality is made up of four variables:
"Bravery", "Caring", "Prudence" and "Motivation".
These values fluctuate depending on the orders
you give in combat:

CHARGE:   Bravery up, Prudence slightly down
MIXED:    Prudence up, Caring slightly down
CAUTIOUS: Caring up, Bravery slightly down

Motivation increases when you use any strategy except
COMMAND, and decreases when you use COMMAND.
Running away from combat decreases all your monsters'
Bravery and Motivation considerably.

Personality variables only change when you explicitly
choose a strategy for each monster during combat.
If you skip this step by choosing FIGHT, or if you use
an item, none of your monsters' personalities change.

The higher a monster's level, the more slowly its
personality changes (i.e. fewer points are added or
subtracted to the variables each time you select a
strategy)

A monster's personality type is determined by which
of the first three characteristics are high, low, and
average, as follows:

-------------------

The Motivation parameter cannot be directly observed
short of using a GameShark.

A monster's personality type and family determine what
it says when you talk to it in the room to the left of
the Arena reception.

The first three personality traits determine whether
or not a monster will obey a command.  In general,
monsters prefer to obey commands that suit their
strongest characteristic: Brave monsters prefer CHARGE,
caring ones CAUTIOUS and prudent ones MIXED.  Note that
a monster with low WLD (wildness) will usually obey any
command, and a monster with extremely high WLD won't
obey even a command suited to its personality.

"Motivation" affects the ease of raising the other three
personality traits.  High "Motivation" causes traits to
increase more rapidly and decrease more slowly.

Finally, each personality trait relates to two special
combat actions.  There are two types of "personal actions":
positive and negative.  The positive actions occur more
often the higher a particular trait is, and their effect
is to enhance the monster's normal action in some way.
The negative actions occur more often the lower a trait is,
and replace the monster's intended action with a less
effective (but free) version of a defensive skill.

High Bravery:    Critical hit with any physical attack
High Caring:     Use a healing skill without spending MP
High Prudence:   Status attack ignores the victim's resistance
High Motivation: 50% additional damage with any attack

Low Bravery:    Like "StrongD", but less powerful
Low Caring:     Like "Dodge", but an ally always takes the hit
Low Prudence:   Like "BladeD", but only counters one attack
Low Motivation: Like "SideStep", but only evades one attack


 B R E E D I N G
-----------------

In the following chart, names in CAPITAL LETTERS in
square brackets indicate monster families.  You can
substitute any monster in the appropriate family.

A series of names enclosed in braces means you can sub-
stitute any of the monsters within that set of braces.

Some monsters have several completely separate breeding
combinations, some of which may allow alternates for
one or both parents.  I'll try to explain how to read
one of the more complex entries:

          /Skullgon \
          |DeadNoble|
          |WhiteKing| [DRAGON   ]
          |BoneSlave|
          \Skeletor /
                      /Swordgon \
                      |Rayburn  |
                      |Andreal  |
Skullgon  [ZOMBIE   ] |GreatDrak|
                      |Orochi   |
                      |BattleRex|
                      \Divinegon/
                      /Skullgon \
          [DRAGON   ] |DeadNoble|
                      |WhiteKing|
                      \Servant  /

This entry shows that there are three different ways to
breed a Skullgon:  You can breed a Skullgon, DeadNoble,
WhiteKing, BoneSlave or Skeletor, with a member of the
Dragon family.  Or you can breed a member of the Zombie
family with a Swordgon, Rayburn, Andreal, GreatDrak,
Orochi, BattleRex or Divinegon.  Or as a third method,
you can breed a member of the Dragon family with a
Skullgon, DeadNoble, WhiteKing or Servant.  Note where
each pair of braces begins and ends.  You can't breed
a DRAGON with a Divinegon to get a Skullgon; those species
are part of two separate combinations.

If a match of monsters could fall under two or more
different combinations, the game chooses the combination
to use in the following order:  First, a species + species
combo is used if one exists.  Second, a species + family
combo is selected.  Third, a family + species combo, and
finally, a family + family combination if no more specific
combination is found.

For example, a match of an Ogre and a LizardMan could
result in either an ArcDemon (Ogre + [DRAGON]), a Lionex
([DEVIL] + LizardMan) or a MedusaEye ([DEVIL] + [DRAGON]).
In fact, the result of this match will be an ArcDemon
because the species + family combination has higher
priority than the family + species or family + family
combinations.

Parent1 is the "pedigree" parent.  When breeding your own
monsters, the one you select first determines the pedigree.
When breeding with another player via Game Link, each
player's monster determines the pedigree for the egg that
player recieves (breeding with an NPC works the same way:
the monster you contribute is the pedigree parent)

When breeding your own monsters, the Plus of an offspring
is equal to the higher plus of the two parents + 1.
When breeding with another player or NPC, the plus of your
egg is equal to the plus of your own monster + 1.

In either case, the sum of the parents' levels increases
the Plus as follows:

Lv    Bonus
-----------
20-39   0
40-59   1
60-75   2
76-99   3
100+    4

A few specific breeding combinations result in an additional
+2 on the offspring.

Each statistic score of a newly-hatched, level 1 monster,
including personality characteristics, is equal to one-half
the mean of its parents' scores.

from the battle AI to the random map generation,
is thoroughly dissected in almost agonizing detail.

chosen Japanese as an elective course.

games.  He's also famous as a member of J2E
Translations, the group who brought us the original,
uncensored Final Fantasy IV in English.  What can
I say, this guy knows his s*** with a hex editor.
This guide isn't the first project Necrosaro's
helped me with, either...
